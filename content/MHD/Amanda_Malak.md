Dr. Amanda Malak (she/her/we/us) is a psychologist and founder of Malak Psychology, a private practice located on the ancestral lands of the Xalychidom Piipash and Akimel O'Odham peoples (Scottsdale, AZ). She provides telehealth psychotherapy to adolescents and adults across Arizona, New York, and all PSYPACT states, with occasional support for clients internationally. <br>
Dr. Malak specializes in serving LGBTQ+ and BIPOC populations, with clinical focuses that include multicultural couples and families, faith transitions, perinatal and postpartum mental health, autism, ADHD, and intergenerational trauma. She has particular expertise in couples and family therapy with LGBTQ+ individuals navigating conservative cultural or religious backgrounds, supporting clients and their families across geographic distances. Her therapeutic approach draws from a range of evidence-based modalities, including CBT, CPT, ACT, DBT, family systems, somatic experiencing, and psychodynamic therapy. <br>
Her training spans a broad range of settings, including university counseling, community mental health, forensic assessment and therapy, perinatal mental health, substance use counseling, police psychology, and work with seriously mentally ill populations. While conducting psychological assessments at Malak Psychology, she is currently contracted with Time to Evaluate, an organization that integrates AI into autism assessment across NY and PSYPACT states, and Josi Health who does autism and ADHD evaluations virtually while accepting many major insurances. <br>
On a personal note, Dr. Malak is a heterosexually married bisexual woman, neurodivergent, a first-generation American of Coptic Egyptian heritage, and a mother. She is deeply committed to expanding mental health access for Southwest Asian and North African (SWANA) communities both in the US and abroad. <br>
