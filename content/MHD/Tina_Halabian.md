Dr. Tina Halabian, PsyD is a licensed clinical psychologist with a doctorate from Loma Linda University. She completed her predoctoral residency at Yale University and her postdoctoral fellowship at New York University. Dr. Halabian has advanced training in evidence-based treatments, including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT), and Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP) for anxiety disorders and obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD), through UCLA. <br>
She takes a lifespan-oriented approach and has advanced training and experience working with children, adolescents, young adults, families, and couples. Dr. Halabian treats anxiety and mood disorders, including generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), OCD, and depression, as well as overlapping concerns related to relationships, life transitions, family dynamics, and stress. Her therapeutic style is warm, collaborative, and nonjudgmental, while also being appropriately challenging. She has trained and practiced across California, Connecticut, and New York and provides culturally informed care in both English and Farsi (Persian). <br>
