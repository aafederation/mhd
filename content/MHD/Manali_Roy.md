I am Manali Roy, Ph.D., a licensed clinical psychologist licensed to practice in New York State. I was born and raised in India and moved to the United States as a young adult for advanced education. Having lived in two countries and cultures for significant parts of my life has shaped a unique, multicultural perspective and associated challenges and rewards. My clinical experience spans community mental health and city hospitals in both India and the U.S., where I have worked extensively with underserved and multicultural communities. Multicultural concerns, minority and immigrant experiences, intergenerational trauma, social and racial inequities, acculturative stress, concerns of gender identities and sexual orientation, and the intersectionality of identities are central to my work. I am also particularly committed to supporting South Asian LGBTQ+ individuals. I aim to offer culturally sensitive therapy that fosters cross‑cultural understanding and promotes mental health equity.\ <br>
I work with individuals navigating anxiety, depression, trauma, neurodivergence, acculturative stress, and life transitions. My goal is to create a safe, collaborative space where your experiences are heard and understood. Together, we explore your concerns in depth, identify challenges and unhelpful patterns in how you relate to yourself, others, and the world, recognize your strengths, and work toward enhancing psychological well‑being through practical coping skills and strategies. ​ <br>
​I provide therapy in English, Bengali, and Hindi. <br>
