Shreeja Vachhani, LMSW, is a psychotherapist at Transcendent Self Therapy in Williamsburg, Brooklyn. She provides individual therapy for adolescents and adults navigating anxiety, trauma, relationship and family-of-origin challenges, identity exploration, life transitions, perfectionism, emotional overwhelm, and disconnection from themselves or others. She also offers couples therapy for partners seeking to better understand recurring relational patterns, strengthen communication, and build deeper connection. <br>
Her approach is relational, psychodynamic, trauma-informed, and grounded in attachment-focused, somatic, and mindfulness-based approaches to therapy. As an Indian-American therapist, Shreeja brings a culturally sensitive and systems-aware lens to her work, informed by both lived experience and ongoing learning around bicultural identity, intergenerational dynamics, and decolonized approaches to healing and mental health. <br>
Shreeja received her Master of Social Work from the University of Michigan-Ann Arbor and previously completed a Post-MSW Fellowship at the Mary A. Rackham Institute Psychological Clinic at the University of Michigan. She provides therapy in English, Hindi, and Gujarati. <br>
